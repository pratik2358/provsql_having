\section{Semantics}
\label{sec:semantics}

In this section we give a general \emph{possible-world semantics} for
selection predicates involving comparisons of aggregate values, in
arbitrary m-semirings.

In the approach presented in~\cite{amsterdamer2011provenance}, also used
in the ProvSQL implementation \cite{sen2026provsql},
annotation of aggregate query results rely on a \emph{semimodule} whose
elements are tensor products of data values and semiring elements, with
the aggregation operation, required to be a commutative monoid operation,
being lifted as the semimodule sum. This allows compositionality and some
form of compatibility between semiring operations and the aggregate
values. On the other hand, this rules out
common non-associative operations (such as \sql{AVG}) or non-commutative ones
(such as \fakesql{ARRAY\_AGG} or \fakesql{LISTAGG}). In contrast, for
generality and because our semantics does not depend on the
semimodule structure, we extend to any arbitrary aggregate function on
\emph{sequences} of values. This amounts to replacing the semimodule
with a formal non-commutative algebraic structure.

We fix a commutative m-semiring
$(\mathbb{K},\oplus,\otimes,\ominus,\mathbb{0},\mathbb{1})$, as discussed in the preliminaries, along with a data domain $\mathcal{V}$.
For any set~$S$, we write $\Seq(S)$ the set of finite sequences over $S$.
We define aggregate functions and comparison operators:
\begin{definition}
An \emph{aggregate function} is a function  $f:\Seq(\mathcal{V})\to\mathcal{V}$.
  For any comparison operator $\op\in\{<,\leq,=,\geq,>,\neq\}$ defined
  over $\mathcal{V}$, i.e., $\op: \mathcal{V}^2\rightarrow
  \{\bot,\top\}$, we denote
\(
  \chi_{\op}:\mathcal{V}\times\mathcal{V}\to \mathbb{K}
  \) as the mapping that sends $(a,b)$ to $\mathbb{1}$ if $a\op b$, and to
  $\mathbb{0}$ otherwise.
\end{definition}

Sen et al.~\cite{sen2026provsql} provide a provenance semantics over annotated
relations for the multiset relational alegbra extended with
\emph{terminal} aggregation,
itself based on that of
\cite{green2007provenance,geerts2010database,amsterdamer2011provenance}.
We extend the semantics of the \emph{selection} operator to allow
comparisons of aggregate values (i.e., non-terminal aggregates) using
possible-world semantics. As we allow non-commutative aggregation
functions, we also need to specify, along with the aggregation function,
which ordering is used,
similarly to the ``\sql{agg(...) WITHIN GROUP (ORDER BY ...)}'' standard SQL
feature, or the ``\sql{agg(... ORDER BY ...)}'' feature of PostgreSQL.
This ordering is irrelevant for commutative aggregates and can be chosen
arbitrarily in that case. We define possible worlds of a multiset of
tuples under such an ordering:
\begin{definition}\label{def:possible_world}
  Let $M$ be a multiset of annotated tuples over
  $\mathcal{V}^k\times\mathbb{K}$ (with $k\in\mathbb{N}$), let
  $(i_j)_{1\leq j \leq m}$ with $1\leq i_j \leq k$ be distinct
  \emph{grouping indices}, and let $\preceq$ be a total order on
  $\mathcal{V}^k$.
For any group $\vec v=(v_1,\dots,v_m)\in\mathcal{V}^m$, we denote by
$U^{\preceq}_{M, \vec v}$, abbreviated~$U$ when $M$, $\preceq$, $\vec v$
are clear from context, the sequence of all tuples $(u,\alpha)\in M$
such that $u_{i_j}=v_j$ for all $j$, taken with their multiplicity and
ordered by $\preceq$ on the value part~$u$ (occurrences of equal
value-tuples being ordered arbitrarily).

  A \emph{possible world}~$W$ of $U$ is a subsequence
  of $U$, denoted $W\sqsubseteq U$. Its \emph{$\mathbb{K}$-annotation} is:
\[
\ann_U(W) \defeq
\Bigl( \bigotimes_{(u,\alpha)\in W} \alpha\Bigr)
 \otimes
\Bigl( \mathbb{1} \ominus \bigoplus_{(u,\alpha)\in U\setminus W}
  \alpha\Bigr)
\]
where $U\setminus W$ denotes the occurrences of $U$ not retained
in~$W$, and empty $\bigotimes$-products (resp.\ $\bigoplus$-sums) equal
$\mathbb{1}$ (resp.\ $\mathbb{0}$).
\end{definition}

We now give the semantics of \emph{selection} over comparison predicates
involving aggregate functions. For simplicity of presentation we first
consider the case where the selection predicate is a single comparison;
the last two clauses below extend it to Boolean combinations of
predicates.

\begin{definition}[Provenance of selection over aggregate comparisons]
\label{def:semantics}
Let $\hat I$ be a $\mathbb K$-instance, $k\in\NN$, $q\in\RA_k$, and let
$\psi$ be a selection predicate over arity~$k$. To $\psi$ we associate a
\emph{predicate provenance} $\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}\colon\mathcal V^k\to\mathbb K$,
defined by induction on the structure of~$\psi$.

\smallskip\noindent\emph{Ordinary comparison.}
If $\psi=(t\op t')$ with $t,t'$ terms of index $\le k$, then
\[
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u)\defeq \chi_{\op}\bigl(t(u),t'(u)\bigr).
\]

\smallskip\noindent\emph{Aggregate comparison.}
If $\psi$ compares aggregate values, then $q$ is a grouping query
$q=\gamma_{i_1,\dots,i_m}[t_1{:}f_1,\dots,t_n{:}f_n](q')$ with
$q'\in\RA_{k'}$, and $\psi=(t:f)\op(t':f')$ or $\psi=(t:f)\op t'$ with
$t,t'$ of index $\le k'$. Fix a total order $\preceq$ sorting each group
(irrelevant when $f,f'$ are commutative). For an output tuple
$u=(\vec v,a_1,\dots,a_n)$ with group key $\vec v=(v_1,\dots,v_m)$, let
$U\defeq U^{\preceq}_{\angsem{\hat I}{q'},\,\vec v}$; then
\[
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u)\defeq
\bigoplus_{\emptyset\neq W\sqsubseteq U}
\ann_U(W)\otimes
\chi_{\op}\!\Bigl(f\bigl((t(u'))_{(u',\alpha')\in W}\bigr),\; g_W\Bigr),
\]
where $g_W\defeq f'\bigl((t'(u'))_{(u',\alpha')\in W}\bigr)$ if
$\psi=(t:f)\op(t':f')$, and $g_W\defeq t'(u)$ if $\psi=(t:f)\op t'$.

\smallskip\noindent\emph{Boolean combinations.}
For selection predicates $\psi_1,\psi_2$ and $u\in\mathcal V^k$,
\[
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_1\land\psi_2}(u)\defeq
  \predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_1}(u)\otimes\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_2}(u),
\quad
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_1\lor\psi_2}(u)\defeq
  \predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_1}(u)\oplus\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_2}(u),
\quad
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\neg\psi}(u)\defeq
  \mathbb 1\ominus\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u).
\]

\smallskip\noindent
The selection $\sigma_\psi(q)$ is then annotated by
\[
\angsem{\hat I}{\sigma_\psi(q)}\defeq
\begin{cases}
\mset{\,(u,\ \alpha\otimes\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u))\mid(u,\alpha)\in\angsem{\hat I}{q}\,}
  & \text{if $\psi$ has no aggregate-comparison atom,}\\[1ex]
\mset{\,(u,\ \predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u))\mid(u,\alpha)\in\angsem{\hat I}{q}\,}
  & \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
\]
\end{definition}
In the second case the annotation $\alpha$ of the grouped tuple is
dropped: the possible-world sum ranges over non-empty worlds only, so it
already enforces group existence (the role of the $\delta(\cdot)$-type
factor $\alpha$), making the extra $\otimes\,\alpha$ redundant.
