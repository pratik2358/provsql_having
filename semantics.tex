\section{Semantics}
\label{sec:semantics}

In this section we give a general \emph{possible-world semantics} for
selection predicates involving comparisons of aggregate values, in
arbitrary m-semirings.

In the approach presented in~\cite{amsterdamer2011provenance}, also used
in the ProvSQL implementation \cite{sen2026provsql},
annotation of aggregate query results rely on a \emph{semimodule} whose
elements are tensor products of data values and semiring elements, with
the aggregation operation, required to be a commutative monoid operation,
being lifted as the semimodule sum. This allows compositionality and some
form of compatibility between semiring operations and the aggregate
values. On the other hand, this rules out
common non-associative operations (such as \sql{AVG}) or non-commutative ones
(such as \fakesql{ARRAY\_AGG} or \fakesql{LISTAGG}). In contrast, for
generality and because our semantics does not depend on the
semimodule structure, we extend to any arbitrary aggregate function on
\emph{sequences} of values. This amounts to replacing the semimodule
with a formal non-commutative algebraic structure.

We fix a commutative m-semiring
$(\mathbb{K},\oplus,\otimes,\ominus,\mathbb{0},\mathbb{1})$, as discussed in the preliminaries, along with a data domain $\mathcal{V}$.
For any set~$S$, we write $\Seq(S)$ the set of finite sequences over $S$.
We define aggregate functions and comparison operators:
\begin{definition}
An \emph{aggregate function} is a function  $f:\Seq(\mathcal{V})\to\mathcal{V}$.
  For any comparison operator $\op\in\{<,\leq,=,\geq,>,\neq\}$ defined
  over $\mathcal{V}$, i.e., $\op: \mathcal{V}^2\rightarrow
  \{\bot,\top\}$, we denote
\(
  \chi_{\op}:\mathcal{V}\times\mathcal{V}\to \mathbb{K}
  \) as the mapping that sends $(a,b)$ to $\mathbb{1}$ if $a\op b$, and to
  $\mathbb{0}$ otherwise.
\end{definition}

Sen et al.~\cite{sen2026provsql} provide a provenance semantics over annotated
relations for the multiset relational alegbra extended with
\emph{terminal} aggregation,
itself based on that of
\cite{green2007provenance,geerts2010database,amsterdamer2011provenance}.
We extend the semantics of the \emph{selection} operator to allow
comparisons of aggregate values (i.e., non-terminal aggregates) using
possible-world semantics. As we allow non-commutative aggregation
functions, we also need to specify, along with the aggregation function,
which ordering is used,
similarly to the ``\sql{agg(...) WITHIN GROUP (ORDER BY ...)}'' standard SQL
feature, or the ``\sql{agg(... ORDER BY ...)}'' feature of PostgreSQL.
This ordering is irrelevant for commutative aggregates and can be chosen
arbitrarily in that case. We define possible worlds of a multiset of
tuples under such an ordering:
\begin{definition}\label{def:possible_world}
  Let $M$ be a multiset of tuples of arity $k+1\in\mathbb{N}$ over
  $\mathcal{V}^k\times\mathbb{K}$, and
$(i_j)_{1\leq j \leq m}$ s.t. $1\leq
i_j \leq k$ be distinct \emph{grouping indices}.
For any total order relation $\preceq$ over $\mathcal{V}$ and for
and any group $\vec v=(v_1,\dots,v_m)\in\mathcal{V}^m$, we consider
the sequence of all tuples of $M$ agreeing on coordinate $i_j$ with $v_j$
for all $j$, with their multiplicity, ordered by $\preceq$, which we
denote
\(
  U^{\preceq}_{M, \vec v}
  \).

  A \emph{possible world}~$W$ of $U^{\preceq}_{M, \vec v}$ is a subsequence
  of $U^\preceq_{M, \vec v}$. For any such possible world $W$, its
  \emph{$\mathbb{K}$-annotation} is:
\[
\ann(W) \defeq
\left( \bigotimes_{(u,\alpha)\in W} \alpha\right)
 \otimes
\left( \mathbb{1} \ominus \bigoplus_{(u,\alpha)\in W} \alpha\right)
\]
where empty $\bigotimes$-products equal $\mathbb{1}$ and empty $\bigoplus$-sums equal $\mathbb{0}$.
\end{definition}

We now formalize a general semantics for \emph{selection} over comparison
predicates involving aggregate functions; we first consider for
simplicity of presentation the case where the selection predicate is a
single comparison.
\begin{definition}
Fix a $\mathbb K$-instance $\hat I$  and let $q\in \RA_k$.
A \emph{selection predicate} $\psi$ over arity $k$ induces a function
  $\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi} : \mathcal V^k \to \mathbb{K}$,
defined by recursion on~$\psi$ as follows:

\smallskip\noindent
For an atomic comparison $\psi$ of the form $t\op t'$:
\[
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u)\defeq \chi_{\op}(t(u),t'(u))
\]
\smallskip\noindent
Otherwise, $q$ is of the form $\gamma_{i_1,\dots,i_m}[t_1:f_1,\dots,t_n:f_n](q')\in \RA_{k=m+n}$
with $q'\in\RA_{k'}$, distinct grouping indices $(i_j)_{1\le j\le m}$ such that $1\le i_j\le k'$,
and $\psi=(t:f)\ \op\ (t':f')$ or $\psi=(t:f)\ \op\ t'$ where max-index of $t,t'\leq k'$.

\smallskip\noindent

\smallskip\noindent
For $\psi = (t:f) \op (t':f')$,
\[
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u)\defeq
\bigoplus_{\substack{W\subseteq [|U^{\hat I}_{q',\vec v}|]\\ W\neq\emptyset}}
\Bigl(
\ann^{\hat I}_{q',\vec v}(W)\otimes
\chi_{\op}\bigl(f(\pi_t(((U^{\hat I}_{q',\vec v})_r[u])_{r\in W}^\omega)),\ f'(\pi_{t'}(((U^{\hat I}_{q',\vec v})_r[u])_{r\in W}^\omega)) \bigr)
\Bigr)
\]

\smallskip\noindent
and for $\psi = (t:f) \op t'$,
\[
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u)\defeq
\bigoplus_{\substack{W\subseteq [|U^{\hat I}_{q',\vec v}|]\\ W\neq\emptyset}}
\Bigl(
\ann^{\hat I}_{q',\vec v}(W)\otimes
\chi_{\op}\bigl(f(\pi_t(((U^{\hat I}_{q',\vec v})_r[u])_{r\in W}^\omega)),\ t'(u)\bigr)
\Bigr)
\]
where, for $u=(\vec v,a_1,\dots,a_n)\in\mathcal V^{m+n}$ ,
we write $\vec v=(v_1,\dots,v_m)$ for the group key.


\smallskip\noindent
Now, for $u\in\mathcal V^k$, let $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$ be arbitrary selection predicates. Then,
\[
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_1\land \psi_2}(u)\defeq
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_1}(u)\otimes \predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_2}(u),
\]
\[
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_1\lor \psi_2}(u)\defeq
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_1}(u)\oplus \predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi_2}(u),
\]
\[
\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\neg \psi}(u)\defeq \mathbb 1 \ominus \predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u).
\]

\end{definition}


\begin{definition}[Provenance for \sql{HAVING}-like queries]Let $\hat I$ be a $\mathbb K$-instance, let $k\in\NN$, let $q\in\RA_k$, and let $\psi$ be
a well-formed selection predicate over arity $k$.
We define:
% \[
% \angsem{\hat I}{\sigma_\psi(q)} \defeq
% \mset{\, (u,\alpha\otimes \predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u)) \mid (u,\alpha)\in \angsem{\hat I}{q}\,}.
% \]
%
\[
\angsem{\hat I}{\sigma_\psi(q)} \defeq
\begin{cases}
\mset{\, (u,\alpha\otimes \predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u)) \mid (u,\alpha)\in \angsem{\hat I}{q}\,}
& \text{if $\psi$ contains no aggregate-comparison atom,}\\[1ex]
\mset{\, (u,\predsem{\hat I}{q}{\psi}(u)) \mid (u,\alpha)\in \angsem{\hat I}{q}\,}
& \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
\]\label{def:semantics}
\end{definition}
We note that this possible-world semantics
ranges over non-empty worlds only. So multiplying the possible-world provenance expression with $\alpha$ (which is a $\delta(\cdot)$-type provenance expression to enforce group existence) is redundant.
